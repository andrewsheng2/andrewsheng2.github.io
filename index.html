About Me

I am currently residing in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania and am a current second-year computer science student studying at Carnegie Mellon University, but I am from Brooklyn, New York. I am primarily focused on artificial intelligence and machine learning, and am planning on pursuing a machine learning concentration and physics minor. I primarily code in Python, C, and SML/NJ but am interested in other languages such as React.js.

I am also passionate about audio engineering, and am currently a part of AB Tech, an organization that provides sound and lighting support to many events at Carnegie Mellon. I am primarily a sound engineer, but am also interested in lighting design, and various other positions in entertainment technology. See the top of this website for some of the shows that I have worked on!

Here are some of my past work experiences:

AB Tech (Pittsburgh, PA)

Techie and Assistant Personnel Manager, September 2018 to Present

AB Tech is a student organization at Carnegie Mellon University that provides professional-grade sound and lighting support for many events around campus. I usually serve as a sound engineer for many events, where I ensure that we use the correct selection of microphones and speakers for the best audience listening experience, but I occasionally also serve as a production manager, where I communicate with organizers to make sure we meet their technical needs, to help plan what positions are needed for the show, and to set deadlines for crucial information such as set lists. I also serve as the Assistant Personnel Manager for the organization, where I facilitated smooth operation of the organization by ensuring all events have enough people assigned to work on them.

CPC Beacon (Brooklyn, NY)

Youth Worker (Teaching Assistant), Summers 2018 and 2019

CPC Beacon is a nonprofit organization in Brooklyn, NY that provides education for lower-income and immigrant families. During the summers of 2018 and 2019, I served as a youth worker for the organization, where I led lessons primarily in science and math. For example, I introduced and got students interested in computer science by teaching engaging lessons involving computer graphics. I also developed a point system implemented using Python to encourage students to participate in classroom activities and to show a real-world use of computer science. In addition to teaching lessons, I was also involved with the camp's annual summer showcase, running the sound system during the showcase as well as communicating and assisting most classes with audio editing to make sure the showcase ran smoothly and professionally.
